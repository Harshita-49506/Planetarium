Birth of Clary and her exploration with Bea

Bea: Hey, there, little one... I am you, but older. Don't be scared. I won't harm you. What's your name?
Clary: Wh.. Who a.. are you??
Clary: I don't know. What's yours? Who am I? Who are you? And where are we?
Clary: Oh ok, but what's CME?? and how long have you been here??
Bea: Oh well, I am Bea. I am just like you; we are the CMEs. We are at a star whose name is Sun. It's the star around which all the planets revolve in this Solar System. CME is short for Coronal Mass Ejection. When the Sun's magnetic field gets tangled, it snaps its two ends to reconnect. This releases an enormous amount of energy, ejecting plasma and magnetic fields into space as a CME.

So, basically, the Sun is made of hydrogen and helium particles. At the very core of the Sun, a nuclear reaction takes place in which two hydrogen particles fuse to make helium. Because of this, the temperature gets so high, and we have so many charged particles. All of this together results in the 4th stage of matter, which is plasma. Due to this plasma, a magnetic field is formed around the Sun. When this magnetic field gets tangled and snaps to get itself back in order, a massive burst of solar plasma and magnetic field from the Sun's outer atmosphere, called the corona, happens, which results in the creation of us, Coronal Mass Ejections!

Clary: So I am like a burst of energy made from the Sun!
Bea: Yes, you are a plasma cloud which is full of energy. Oh, how about we name you Clary?
Clary: That sounds great! You look quite big, how long have you been here?
Bea: I came a couple of days before you. You will also increase in size with time. I am just waiting for the solar wind to drift me off from here towards the outer universe or some other planets. The Sun has been quite cold for the past few hours. And the solar storm I was born from didn't have enough energy to drift me off along with my friends.
Clary: What is that??

A solar storm is a sudden explosion of particles, energy, magnetic fields, and material blasted into the solar system by the Sun. The Sun creates a tangled mess of magnetic fields which when snaps and reconnects releases large amounts of energy. These powerful eruptions can generate a bright flash of light called a solar flare, a radiation storm, or an enormous cloud of solar material, called a coronal mass ejection, that is US.

Bea: looks like we are somewhere near venus.
Clary: Venus?? and what is that rock over there??
Bea: Venus is the second planet from sun and that rock is Earth third planet from sun.
Clary: oh well looks like we are gonna collide into it.
Bea: well everything which has come to life has to leave one day.
Clary: What are you thinking ??
Bea: What is going to happen next ??
Clary: Well we both have enough energy to reach Earth. We are going to go there.
Bea: I don't know, I just don't feel well.
Clary: is that so ??
Bea: Yes Clary. Earth's magnetic field works in a similar way to the Sun's - they're both made by moving electric charges inside. But the Sun is extremely hot, while Earth's surface is much cooler which makes it safer for life to exist. Clary, it will hurt a lil. When we collide but then everything will better.

The Satellites ACE & WIND have detected CMEs coming towards the earth. Their speed looks really fast. Issue warning for blackouts at minimum 12 hours from now. We might see some beautiful Auroras tonight.

IMPACT: 12:00:00
WARNING: GEOMAGNETIC STORM DETECTED

As they get near to the earth. The charged particles in their cloud started to pour into the magnetosphere, The magnetic field of Earth. As the reaction started the magnetosphere started compressing, and shaking, which triggered the geomagnetic storm. Charged particles funnel down Earth's magnetic field lines toward the poles. They collide with atmospheric gases, creating auroras- bright, colorful lights in the sky, usually near the Arctic and Antarctic. As the sky glowed with green, blue, and pink lights, Bea and Clary's charged particles danced together in the air-safe again, happy, and hopping with joy. Lisa woke up as the light from Clary, Bea, and countless other CME particles touched her face. She smiled at the sky, eyes half-asleep, admiring the glow as if it were all just a dream.
